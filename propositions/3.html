For two given unequal straight-lines, to cut off from the greater a straight-line equal to the lesser. --- Let AB and C be the two given unequal straight-lines, of which let the greater be AB. So it is required to cut off a straight-line equal to the lesser from the greater AB. --- Let the line AD, equal to the straight-line C, have been placed at point . And let the circle have been drawn with center A and radius AD [Post. 3]. --- And since point A is the center of circle DEF, is equal to AD [Def. 1.15]. But, C is also equal to AD. Thus, [question 4] and C are each equal to AD. So AE is also equal to C. --- Thus, for two given unequal straight-lines, AB and C, the (straight-line) AE, equal to the lesser C, has been cut off from the greater AB. (Which is) the very thing it was required to do. ---
